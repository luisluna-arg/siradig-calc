# Document Writing Skill

## Principles
* **Pyramid Principle:** Lead with the most important information; follow with details.
* **Scan-First Design:** Use headers, bolding, and lists to ensure the document is readable at a glance.
* **Clarity over Cleverness:** Use simple words, active voice, and avoid conversational filler.

## Structural Requirements
* **Standard Headers:** Use Title Case for H1/H2, Sentence case for H3+.
* **Scoping:** Always include a "Context" or "TL;DR" section at the top.
* **Non-Goals:** For technical specs, explicitly state what is NOT being addressed to prevent scope creep.
* **Callouts:** Use blockquotes for warnings or critical notes.

## Style Constraints
* No introductory fluff (e.g., "In this document, we will...").
* No empathetic or conversational padding.
* Use parallel construction in all bulleted lists.
* Tables must be used for comparisons involving more than two variables.

## Execution
1. Identify the target audience (technical vs. non-technical).
2. Outline the hierarchy of information.
3. Apply the formatting standards above to the drafted content.
